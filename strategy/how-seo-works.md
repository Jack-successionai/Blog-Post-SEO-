# How SEO Works — For the Succession AI Team

## The core loop

1. **Someone types something into Google** — e.g. "how much is my business worth"
2. **Google shows results** — ranked by relevance, authority, content quality
3. **If we have a blog post** that answers that query well, Google ranks us
4. **That person clicks our link** — free traffic, no ad spend
5. **They land on our site** → read our content → see our valuation tool → become a lead

The whole game: **figure out what our audience Googles, then write content that answers those searches better than anyone else.**

## Key terms

| Term | What it means | Why it matters |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| **Search volume** | How many times/month people Google this exact phrase | Size of the opportunity. 1,900/mo = 1,900 people asking this question every month. |
| **Keyword difficulty (KD)** | Score 0-100 measuring how hard it is to rank on page 1 | 0-15 = easy (good content alone works). 50+ = avoid (dominated by massive sites). |
| **CPC (cost per click)** | What advertisers pay per click for Google Ads on this keyword | Indicates commercial intent. $16/click means high-value searchers ready to act. $0 = just browsing. |
| **CTR (click-through rate)** | % of searchers who click a result | Position 1 gets ~30% of clicks. Position 5 gets ~6%. |
| **SERP** | Search Engine Results Page — the Google results page | We analyze who currently ranks to understand competition. |
| **Backlinks** | Other websites linking to our content | Google treats each link as a "vote of confidence." More quality links = higher rankings. |

## How Google decides who ranks

1. **Relevance** — Does the page actually answer the search query?
2. **Content quality** — Is it comprehensive, well-structured, and useful?
3. **Authority** — Does the domain have credibility? (backlinks, domain age, other quality content)
4. **User experience** — Fast loading, mobile-friendly, easy to navigate

## What a keyword tells us about the searcher

The keyword reveals WHERE they are in their exit journey:

| Stage | What they search | What they need | Our content |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|-------------|
| **1. Awareness** | "succession planning", "business exit strategy" | Education — they know they need to plan but don't know how | Guides, frameworks, templates |
| **2. Valuation** | "business valuation calculator", "SDE vs EBITDA" | Numbers — they want to know what their business is worth | Calculator tool, valuation explainers |
| **3. Active intent** | "sell my business", "how to sell a business" | Process — they've decided to sell and need steps | Step-by-step guides, checklists |
| **4. Tax & structure** | "capital gains exemption", "share sale vs asset sale" | Optimization — they want to keep more of the money | Tax strategy guides, deal structure explainers |

## What makes us different from competitors

Most sites ranking for M&A/succession keywords are:
- **Banks** (BMO, Bank of America) — generic content, not actionable
- **Accounting firms** (BDO, Grant Thornton) — written for other professionals, not founders
- **HR platforms** (AIHR, SHRM) — writing about corporate CEO replacement, not business exit
- **Marketplaces** (BizBuySell, Acquire.com) — focused on listings, not education

We write for **founder-led businesses with $500K-$50M revenue** — practical, plain-language, action-oriented. This is an underserved audience in the content landscape.
