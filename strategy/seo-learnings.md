Patterns and insights extracted from performance data. Updated by `/seo-analyze`. Read by `/seo-next-post` and `/keyword-recommend` to inform content decisions.

## How This Works

Each learning is a pattern backed by evidence. Learnings are tagged:
- **[CONFIRMED]** — Seen across 3+ posts or 2+ snapshots
- **[EMERGING]** — Seen once or twice, needs more data
- **[DISPROVED]** — Was a hypothesis, data says otherwise (kept for reference)

## Content Structure

### [EMERGING] Depth + worked examples drive engagement
- **Evidence:** LCGE post (10 min read, worked dollar examples, embedded calculator) was validated as "really fire" by the team. Matches competitor analysis: Raincatcher's pillar pages with real examples outrank shorter content.
- **Action:** Always include at least one concrete worked example with real dollar amounts.

### [EMERGING] Embedded interactive tools boost time-on-page
- **Evidence:** LCGE post has an embedded tax calculator. **Rybbit data (2026-03-31):** /valuation page has highest time-on-page at 81s vs site average ~50s — 1.6x engagement. Competitor analysis: Empire Flippers increased conversions 51.6% by optimizing their valuation tool.
- **Action:** Where a calculator or tool is relevant, embed it directly in the post (not just link to it). Prioritize driving traffic to /valuation — high engagement but only 22 views/month.

### [EMERGING] FAQ schema may improve CTR
- **Evidence:** All 5 posts have FAQ structured data. No CTR data yet. Competitor analysis: Raincatcher uses FAQ schema and wins featured snippets.
- **Action:** Include 3-5 FAQ entries with schema on every post.

## Keywords & Targeting

### [EMERGING] Canadian tax/regulatory content has low competition
- **Evidence:** LCGE keyword scored 89/100 (WRITE NOW) — high volume (1900 CA), low KD (16). Canadian-specific content faces less competition from US publishers.
- **Action:** Prioritize Canada-specific tax and regulatory keywords.

## Traffic Sources

### [EMERGING] Email campaigns drive highest-quality traffic
- **Evidence:** Rybbit data (2026-03-31): mail.google.com referrals have 16.7% bounce rate vs site average 60.4%. Small sample (6 sessions) but dramatically better engagement.
- **Action:** Monitor email referral quality over next 2 snapshots. If confirmed, prioritize email distribution for new blog posts.

### [EMERGING] successionontario.ca is high-volume but low-quality referrer
- **Evidence:** Rybbit data (2026-03-31): 19.5% of referral traffic but 73.2% bounce rate (highest among major referrers). May indicate mismatched expectations.
- **Action:** Investigate what pages successionontario.ca links to. Consider if landing page can be better aligned with referral intent.
